Calculating Mulch for a 50 Square Metre Area

Fifty square metres represents a substantial landscaping project that might encompass the entire garden bed area of a typical suburban home. This could be your combined front and back garden beds, a large feature garden around a pool area, or multiple smaller beds totalling this size. At the standard 75mm depth recommended for most garden applications, you'll need approximately 3.75 cubic metres of wood chip mulch to cover this area effectively.

The weight of 3.75 cubic metres of wood chip mulch comes to around 1.50 tonnes (1,500 kilograms). This is a meaningful quantity that puts you well into bulk delivery territory. Most landscape suppliers offer their best per-cubic-metre rates at volumes like this, making it an economical purchase compared to buying in smaller quantities.

Delivery Considerations for This Volume

At nearly four cubic metres, you'll want to think carefully about where your supplier can deposit the mulch. A pile this size will occupy roughly a two-metre square area and stand about a metre high. Ideally, have the truck tip the load as close to your garden beds as practical to minimise wheelbarrow trips. Some suppliers offer a boom truck service that can place the mulch directly over fences if access is limited through the property.

Spreading a Large Area Efficiently

For 50 square metres, consider breaking the area into zones and working systematically. Start by distributing the mulch in small piles across the entire area before raking to depth. This prevents material fatigue from repeated long-distance wheelbarrow runs. With an area this size, you might spread the work across a weekend or enlist a helper. A consistent 75mm depth throughout will ensure your plants receive the moisture retention and weed suppression benefits mulch provides.
