How to Calculate Mulch for Your Garden

Calculating the right amount of mulch for your Australian garden is essential for achieving optimal plant health and minimising waste. Whether you're preparing garden beds, creating pathways, or landscaping a large area, our mulch calculator takes the guesswork out of your project planning.

Understanding Mulch Measurements

In Australia, mulch is typically sold by the cubic metre (m³). To calculate how much you need, you'll multiply your area's length by width by depth. Our calculator converts your measurements instantly and provides results in cubic metres, tonnes, and bag quantities, making it easy to order the exact amount you need.

Choosing the Right Mulch Depth

For most Australian garden beds, a depth of 75mm strikes the right balance between weed suppression and moisture retention. If you're mulching around annual flower beds or areas with established plants, 50mm is usually sufficient and allows delicate stems to breathe. For native gardens, steep slopes, or areas where maximum weed control is the priority, go deeper at 100mm. This thicker layer creates a more effective barrier against weeds while providing better insulation for plant roots during temperature extremes.

Types of Mulch Available in Australia

Wood chip mulch at 0.4 tonnes per cubic metre is the most popular all-purpose option, made from chipped tree branches and ideal for pathways where its chunky texture provides excellent longevity. Bark mulch is slightly lighter at 0.35 t/m³ and breaks down slower than wood chips, making it a favourite for formal gardens where appearance matters. Pine bark at 0.3 t/m³ is the lightest option and naturally acidic, which makes it perfect for acid-loving plants like azaleas, camellias, and many Australian natives.

Cypress mulch at 0.45 t/m³ offers natural resistance to termites and fungal decay, making it the smart choice for areas near buildings or in regions with high termite activity. Eucalyptus mulch weighs in at 0.4 t/m³ and carries the distinctive scent of native Australian bush while breaking down to improve your soil quality over time. For vegetable gardens, sugar cane mulch at just 0.2 t/m³ is extremely lightweight and breaks down quickly, adding valuable nutrients back into your soil with each growing season.

Hardwood mulch at 0.5 t/m³ is denser and longer-lasting, perfect for low-maintenance areas where you want mulch that will stay in place for years.

Tips for Applying Mulch

To get the best results from your mulch application, always keep mulch 50-100mm away from plant stems and tree trunks to prevent collar rot and fungal issues. Water the area thoroughly before spreading your mulch, then water again after application to help it settle into place. Since organic mulches break down over time, plan to top up your mulch layer annually to maintain effective coverage. For extra weed suppression in problem areas, consider laying newspaper or cardboard underneath your mulch before spreading—this creates an additional barrier that smothers existing weeds while eventually breaking down to feed your soil.

Ordering and Delivery

Once you've calculated your mulch requirements, you can order from your local landscape supplier. Most suppliers in Australia deliver in bulk by the cubic metre or in bags. For larger projects, bulk delivery is significantly more cost-effective, while bags are convenient for smaller jobs or areas with limited access where a truck can't reach.
